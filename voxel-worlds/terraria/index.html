🎯 What You'll Build

A 2D side-view voxel game in the tradition of Terraria and Starbound. The world is a grid of tiles; the player can mine away tiles, place new ones, and explore a chunked landscape that's bigger than the screen.

🎬 The final game (by the end of the Advanced tier)

  • A scrollable tile world that feels infinite (chunk-streamed)
  • Biome-zoned terrain generated from Perlin/Simplex noise
  • Mine and place tiles under a mouse cursor with a tool reach
  • Foreground and background tile layers (Terraria's wall system)
  • Falling sand & gravel with simple gravity rules
  • Liquids that flow downhill using a cellular automaton
  • Tile-based lighting propagated by BFS flood fill
  • An enemy that pathfinds around your dug-out tunnels
  • Day/night cycle with persistent save/load to localStorage
💡 Why this sub-track first: Terraria's tile model is the shared substrate under almost every 2D voxel game. Once you can think in tile coords, store a world in a Uint8Array, and render a scrollable grid, you have the foundation for liquids, lighting, mining UI, and even Noita-style cellular pixels — they're all just rules over the same grid.

Five tiers, each in its own file so they stay focused and quick to load. Each tier ends with a playable mini-project that becomes the foundation of the next tier.

BEGINNER Ready
Tile Foundations

Understand tile coordinates, store a world in a flat Uint8Array, render it without image assets, scroll a camera, and stack foreground/background layers.

  • What is a 2D voxel world?
  • Tile coordinates ↔ screen pixels
  • 2D array vs flat Uint8Array
  • Drawing a tile grid
  • Procedural textures (no image assets)
  • Camera + viewport culling
  • Foreground vs background walls
INTERMEDIATE Ready
Mining, Placement & Player Physics

Pick a tile under the mouse, click to mine or place, drop a player avatar on the grid, and let gravity-affected tiles fall.

  • Mouse-to-tile picking with tool reach
  • Click-to-mine / click-to-place
  • Tile palette & inventory basics
  • Falling sand & gravel rules
  • AABB player vs. tile-grid collision
  • Mini-project: side-scroll digger
EXPERT Ready

💡 How to Use This Sub-Track

👶 If you're new to game dev

Don't start here — start with the Beginner Fundamentals first, then come back. This sub-track assumes you've seen vectors and a canvas before.

🛠️ If you've made small games before

Start at the Beginner tier of this sub-track. It's the shortest tier — just locking in tile coords, grid storage, and a scrollable camera — but every tier above depends on those fundamentals.

🎮 If you've shipped a Terraria-like before

Skim the Beginner tier as a vocabulary check, then jump to the Expert tier for the worldgen and cellular liquids. The Simulations tier is where the interesting visualizations live.
