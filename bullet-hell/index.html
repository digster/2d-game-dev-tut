🌸 What You'll Build

The repo already teaches the ingredients — vectors, trigonometry, Bézier curves, object pooling, spatial hashing, particles — but it has zero projectile management at scale. This track is that missing piece: a small bullet-hell engine, built one mechanic at a time, that turns those fundamentals into thousands of bullets on screen at 60 fps.

Danmaku ("barrage") is the genre where a wall of enemy fire becomes a beautiful, readable puzzle you thread your ship through. Its whole identity is one idea: your visible ship is big, but the part that can actually be hit is a single pixel-sized dot — and skimming bullets just past it ("grazing") is how you score. We build the player, then the patterns (rings, spirals, aimed fans, rose curves), then a scripted boss with phases and bombs, then make it all run at scale, then add the tooling — a pattern editor and deterministic replay — and the juice.

💡 Patterns are polar equations. A ring of bullets is just θ = i/n · 2π turned into velocities with Vector2D.fromAngle(θ)·speed. Spin that base angle a little each tick and you get a spiral; modulate the radius with cos(kθ) and you get rose-curve petals. Every "advanced trig" topic suddenly has a stunning visual payoff — that's why this genre is the perfect place to apply the math the Fundamentals teach.
