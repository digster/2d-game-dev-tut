🎯 What You'll Build

Every track so far has been real-time — a loop that ticks 60 times a second whether you touch the keyboard or not. A roguelike is the opposite: it is turn-based. The world holds its breath until you act, then everything resolves in order. That single change rewires the architecture, and it's why the genre deserves its own track.

We build the classic dungeon crawler one system at a time — a grid and a turn, then a generated dungeon, then sight and pursuit, then items and minds — and every demo is deterministic: type a seed and the same dungeon, the same monsters, and the same unidentified-potion colours come back every time.

🎬 The final game (by the end of the Simulations tier): The Descent

  • A complete playable roguelike in one browser tab, rendered as ASCII glyphs on a canvas
  • Procedurally generated levels — structured rooms up top, organic caves deeper down, themed by depth
  • Recursive-shadowcasting field-of-view with fog of war and remembered map
  • Monsters with minds — sleep, wake on sight, chase via Dijkstra maps, flee when hurt, on an energy/speed scheduler
  • Items as data — inventory, equipment, unidentified potions & scrolls, status effects (poison, confusion, haste)
  • Permadeath, depth, score, and a shareable seed — the "daily challenge" loop
💡 Why a roguelike is the perfect capstone: the repo already taught the pieces in the Fundamentals — procedural generation, shadow casting, A* pathfinding, ECS, state machines and behavior trees. This track is where they finally come together into a game you can lose.

🗺️ Track Roadmap

Five tiers, each in its own file so it stays focused and quick to load. Each tier ends with a playable mini-project that becomes the foundation of the next. The track is being built tier by tier — cards marked Coming soon are on the roadmap but not yet written.

BEGINNER Ready
The Grid & The Turn

The two ideas every roguelike rests on: the world is a grid of tiles, and time advances one turn at a time.

  • Tile grids & ASCII glyph rendering on a canvas
  • The turn loop — turn-based vs real-time, side by side
  • Bump-to-attack: move-or-attack from one keypress
  • A scrolling message log + seeded combat
  • Mini-project: "One Room, One Rat" — a complete micro-loop
INTERMEDIATE Ready
Building the Dungeon

Procedurally generate the map: rooms, corridors, BSP partitioning, and organic random-walk caves.

  • Place non-overlapping rooms & carve floor
  • Connect them with corridors
  • BSP partitioning (the structured classic)
  • Drunkard's walk (the organic alternative)
  • Mini-project: "Explore the Dungeon" — descend the stairs to a new level
ADVANCED Ready
Sight & Pursuit

What can the player see, what can the monsters see, and how do they hunt you across the grid?

  • Line of sight (Bresenham)
  • Recursive shadowcasting field-of-view
  • Fog of war & remembered map
  • Monster pathing (A*) + Brogue-style Dijkstra maps
  • Mini-project: "The Hunt" — stealth & pursuit
EXPERT Ready
Items, Effects & Minds

Items as data, an inventory you can read, status effects that tick, and monsters with real behaviour.

  • Items via ECS — pick up, drop, equip
  • Inventory & equipment UI
  • Status effects + unidentified potions/scrolls
  • Energy/speed turn scheduling + AI variety (FSM + behavior trees)
  • Mini-project: "Armed & Dangerous"
SIMULATIONS Building next
The Whole Dungeon

Cellular-automata caves, depth-based level themes, deterministic seeds — and the full playable game.

  • Cellular-automata cave generation
  • Level themes & generator selection by depth
  • Deterministic seeds & seed sharing (record/replay proof)
  • Grand capstone: "The Descent" — the complete roguelike

📚 Recommended Prerequisites

This track stands on topics from the Fundamentals guide. The deeper into the track you go, the more they pay off — each tier card below lists what helps most.

Before INTERMEDIATE

  • Procedural Generation — rooms & corridors are a different toolkit from continuous noise, but the mindset is the same
  • Tilemap Systems — our dungeon is a tilemap with rules

Before ADVANCED

  • Shadow Casting & Vision — FOV is the omnidirectional cousin of the vision cones taught there
  • A* Pathfinding — monsters path to you with it
  • Raycasting & Line of Sight — the LOS primitive behind "can it see me?"

Before EXPERT

  • Entity Component System — items, effects and actors are entities with components
  • State Machines & Behavior Trees — the two ways our monsters think
