🏰 What You'll Build

The repo already teaches the ingredients — A* pathfinding, flow fields, raycasting, trigonometry, Bézier curves, object pooling and spatial hashing — but only as standalone demos. A tower defense is the genre that turns every one of them into a load-bearing game mechanic: flow fields route a crowd, A* re-routes it when you drop a tower, a raycast tells a tower it can't shoot through a wall, and trig leads a shot to where a creep will be.

We build it one mechanic at a time. First the core loop — a lane, a spawner, a tower that picks a target and fires a projectile, and lives you lose when a creep leaks through. Then tower types and the targeting strategies that make placement a puzzle. Then the hard part: enemies that navigate a maze you build, and crowds that flow across open ground. Then we make it all run at scale, and finally we make it a real, balanced game — waves, economy, upgrades — with a dashboard to tune it.

💡 The defender's core question is "what do I hit, and where will it be?" A tower's target choice (first / strongest / closest) is a one-line strategy swap, and leading a moving creep is just solving |enemyPos + enemyVel·t − tower| = projSpeed·t for the intercept time t — a quadratic. The attacker's core question is the mirror image: "what's the cheapest route to the goal?" — which is A* when the map is a maze and a flow field when it's a crowd. Two questions, the whole genre.

🗺️ Track Roadmap

Five tiers, each in its own file so it stays focused and quick to load. Each tier ends with a playable mini-project that feeds the next. All five tiers are complete — from one creep on one lane to the grand capstone, "The Last Stand".

The Path & The Tower

The core loop on a fixed lane: a route, a spawner, a tower that targets and fires, and lives you lose to leaks.

  • Mini-project: "First Line of Defense" — place towers on a budget, survive a wave
INTERMEDIATE Ready
Tower Types & Targeting

Placement becomes a puzzle — data-driven tower kinds, swappable targeting modes, and the trig payoff: leading a moving target.

  • Data-driven tower specs (range / rate / damage / cost)
  • Targeting modes — first / last / closest / strongest
  • Lead-the-target — solving the intercept quadratic
  • Splash (AoE) & slow/frost towers; smooth spline lanes

ADVANCED Ready
Mazing, Flow Fields & Sight

The flagship tier — enemies navigate the map. Towers become obstacles, crowds flow across open ground, and walls block shots.

  • A* on the grid; towers as obstacles → enemies re-route
  • The connectivity guard — you can't fully wall the goal
  • Flow fields — one field, a whole crowd (Bloons-style)
  • Line-of-sight towers (raycasting through walls)
  • Mini-project: "Build Your Maze" — lengthen the route
EXPERT Ready
Ten Thousand Creeps

Performance at scale — object pooling, Struct-of-Arrays, and a spatial hash so towers don't check every creep.

  • Why naive arrays stutter (GC) → the zero-alloc pool
  • Struct-of-Arrays creeps & projectiles in Float32Arrays
  • Spatial hashing for range queries & projectile hits
  • Shared flow-field steering & render batching
  • Mini-project: "Swarm" — thousands of creeps at 60 fps
