🎯 What You'll Build

Every other genre track in this repo is about a world — a dungeon, a race track, a voxel planet. This track is about a character. A platformer lives and dies on how the avatar feels under your thumb, and "feel" is not a vibe — it is a stack of small, specific, learnable techniques: coyote time, jump buffering, variable jump height, apex hangtime, corner correction, wall-jumps, dashes. They are the single most-asked-about topics in 2D gamedev, and until now they had no home here.

We build the platformer one system at a time — solid ground first, then the perfect jump, then a bigger moveset, then a camera and a coat of juice, then the engineering that makes it scale — and at every step the physics runs on a fixed timestep, so the same inputs produce the same motion on a 30 Hz laptop and a 240 Hz monitor alike.

🎬 The final game (by the end of the Simulations tier): Summit

  • A complete playable platformer in one browser tab, rendered with asset-free Canvas2D shapes
  • AABB-on-tilemap collision with slopes, one-way platforms, moving platforms and conveyors
  • The full "game feel" kit — coyote time, jump buffering, variable jump height, apex hangtime, corner correction
  • A real moveset — wall-slide, wall-jump, and a Celeste-style dash with refresh-on-ground
  • A camera that follows with a deadzone + look-ahead, parallax backgrounds, screen shake and squash-and-stretch
  • Built to scale — viewport culling, particle pooling, broad-phase collision and a streamed level — plus a goal and a timer
💡 Why a platformer earns its own track: the repo already taught the ingredients in the Fundamentals — forces & integration, friction, collision detection, state machines, lerp and easing (for the camera), and tilemaps. This track is where they compose into a character that feels right.

🗺️ Track Roadmap

Five tiers, each in its own file so it stays focused and quick to load. Each tier ends with a playable mini-project that becomes the foundation of the next. The Beginner, Intermediate and Advanced tiers are ready; the last two unlock one per commit.

BEGINNER Ready
Ground & Gravity

Solid ground under a falling box: the fixed-timestep loop and AABB-on-tilemap collision, the primitive everything else stands on.

  • The platformer loop — fixed timestep, gravity, integrate velocity → position
  • The player as an AABB; the level as a tile grid
  • Per-axis collision (move X & resolve, then Y) — the primitive
  • Grounded detection + a simple jump; run accel, max speed & friction
  • Mini-project: "First Steps" — run and jump a hand-built level
INTERMEDIATE Ready
Game Feel

The small forgiveness windows and curve tweaks that turn a working jump into one that feels right.

  • Coyote time & jump buffering (the grace windows)
  • Variable jump height (release-to-cut)
  • Apex hangtime + asymmetric jump/fall gravity + fast-fall
  • Corner correction; a player state machine (idle/run/jump/fall/land)
  • Mini-project: "Feel Lab" — every assist on a toggle, raw vs juiced
ADVANCED Ready
Abilities & Moving Geometry

A bigger moveset and the trickier collision cases — walls that move, ground that tilts, platforms you drop through.

  • Wall-slide & wall-jump (with a re-grab lockout)
  • Dash — cooldown, refresh-on-ground, dash-cancel (Celeste-style)
  • One-way platforms (land on top, drop through on down+jump)
  • Slopes (swept collision vs triangle tiles); moving platforms & conveyors
  • Mini-project: a vertical ability & geometry gauntlet
EXPERT Coming soon
Camera, Parallax & Juice

The presentation layer — once the level is bigger than the screen, the camera and the "juice" are what sell it.

  • Camera follow — lerp, deadzone, look-ahead
  • Screen shake & parallax scrolling backgrounds
  • Animation state machine driving the character's shapes/frames
  • Particles (run dust, landing puffs), squash-and-stretch, hitstop
  • Mini-project: a scrolling, juicy level
SIMULATIONS Coming soon
Performance, Scale & The Whole Game

The engineering that lets all of the above run on a big level at a smooth frame rate — and the grand capstone that uses everything.

  • Viewport culling — update/draw only what's near the camera
  • Object pooling for particles; broad-phase collision (spatial grid)
  • Chunked / streamed big levels + offscreen render caching
  • Grand capstone: "Summit" — the complete playable platformer
