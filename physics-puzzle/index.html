🎯 What You'll Build

The repo already teaches the ingredients of game physics — gravity and integration, collision, springs, Verlet integration, rigid bodies, fluids — but until now they lived as standalone simulation demos with no game to call home. This track is that home. We build a small 2D physics engine and use it to make the three classics of the genre: a slingshot (Angry Birds), a rope you cut (Cut the Rope), and a contraption you build (World of Goo) — then push on into destruction and fluids.

Every step runs on a fixed timestep, so a launched ball follows the exact same arc on a 30 Hz laptop and a 240 Hz monitor — which is also why the slingshot can draw a reliable dotted trajectory: the preview is just the simulation, run ahead.

💡 One world, two kinds of physics. This track quietly teaches a deep idea: there are two families of 2D physics that live in the same world. Position-based (Verlet) physics — used for ropes, cloth and soft bodies — and impulse-based (velocity) physics — used for rigid bodies that bounce and spin. The grand capstone makes them cooperate. We'll flag the difference as we go.

🎬 The final game (by the end of the Simulations tier): Rube

  • A complete chain-reaction puzzle in one browser tab, asset-free Canvas2D shapes
  • A slingshot launch with drag-to-aim and a live trajectory preview
  • A rope you cut at the right moment so a payload swings free
  • Rigid bodies that tumble — convex shapes that stack, lean, and topple realistically
  • A wall that fractures into debris when hit hard enough
  • Water that pours and floats the goal object across the finish — the whole machine firing in sequence

🗺️ Track Roadmap

Five tiers, each in its own file so it stays focused and quick to load. Each tier maps to a genre and ends with a playable mini-puzzle that feeds the next. The Beginner, Intermediate and Advanced tiers are live; the rest land one commit at a time.

BEGINNER Ready
Launch & Land (Angry Birds)

The physics World, a slingshot, and the bounces that knock a stack down — the foundation the whole track stands on.

  • The World & gravity — bodies, semi-implicit Euler, the fixed loop
  • Walls, ground & restitution — a bounce is reflect-and-attenuate
  • The slingshot — drag to aim, release applies an impulse
  • Trajectory prediction; circle–circle momentum exchange
  • Mini-project: "Knock-Down" — clear a target zone in 3 shots
INTERMEDIATE Ready
Ropes & Chains (Cut the Rope)

Position-based Verlet physics and distance constraints — and the genre's core verb: the cut.

  • Verlet vs Euler; the distance constraint (relaxation)
  • Build a rope, pin it to anchors; swinging & momentum
  • The cut — sever a constraint and the rope falls free
  • Mini-project: "Deliver" — cut to swing a payload onto the goal
ADVANCED Ready
Rigid Bodies & Joints (World of Goo)

The big one — rotation. Convex shapes that spin, stack, lean and topple, linked by breakable joints.

  • Rotation — angular velocity, moment of inertia, torque
  • SAT collision + contact manifolds; impulse resolution with friction
  • Resting stacks (positional correction); joints & breakable joints
  • Mini-project: "Contraption" — a see-saw catapult puzzle
EXPERT Planned
Destruction & Debris

Break things on purpose — impact thresholds, Voronoi fracture, pooled debris, and the juice that sells it.

  • Impact thresholds; Voronoi pre-fracture patterns
  • Spawn debris + object pooling; structural stress / keystone collapse
  • Juice — dust, screen shake, hitstop
  • Mini-project: "Demolition" — collapse a structure on a budget
SIMULATIONS Planned
Soft Bodies, Ragdolls & Fluids

The engineering to run hundreds of bodies smoothly — and the grand capstone that uses every mechanic at once.

  • Spatial-hash broadphase; sleeping bodies & contact islands
  • Pressure soft-body blobs; ragdolls (jointed rigid bodies)
  • SPH fluid + buoyancy — pour water to float an object
  • Grand capstone: "Rube" — the full chain reaction

📚 Recommended Prerequisites

This track stands on topics from the Fundamentals guide and the existing simulation demos — each tier below lists what helps most. You can absolutely start cold; these just deepen the "why".

Before BEGINNER

  • Physics Systems with Forces — gravity, velocity, and integrating motion against dt
  • Collision Detection — circle-vs-circle overlap is the test our bounces are built from
  • Vectors — position, velocity and impulse are all just 2D vectors

For the later tiers

  • Springs & Verlet — the basis for the Intermediate rope tier
  • Rigid Bodies & SAT (simulation-v2) — the rotation + collision math the Advanced tier applies
  • SPH Fluids (simulation-v2) & Buoyancy (simulation) — the Simulations water puzzles
